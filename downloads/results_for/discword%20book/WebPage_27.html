About the Author

TERRY PRATCHETT is the acclaimed creator of the global bestselling Discworld series, the first of which, The Colour of Magic, was published in 1983. Raising Steam is his fortieth Discworld novel. His books have been widely adapted for stage and screen, and he is the winner of multiple prizes, including the Carnegie Medal, as well as being awarded a knighthood for services to literature. After falling out with his keyboard he now talks to his computer. Occasionally, these days, it answers back.

4.0 out of 5 starsIn which the railway of the Disc is described.
January 23, 2015
Format: Hardcover|Verified Purchase
"Mrs. Bradshaw's Handbook" is a charming adjunct to Terry Pratchett's "Raising Steam". Mrs. Bradshaw is mentioned only twice in "Raising Steam", as an aspiring author of travelogs. This book is the travelog she authored; an account of the town along the Ankh Morpork Hygienic Railway. Descriptions of the towns and sights along the lines, and advice for the rail traveller, as provided by an educated and adventurous widow from the great city of Ankh Morpork.

I liked the descriptions and asides, with numerous hints for directions stories set on the Disc World may go next. No plot, to speak of, but still a lot of fun for a Pratchett fan.

The Nightgaunt
4.0 out of 5 starsNot a great story, but rather a nice little addition to your Discworld library
August 18, 2015
Format: Hardcover|Verified Purchase
Don't expect a deep Discworld novel from Mrs' Bradshaw's Handbook. Instead, expect the kind of pleasant yet not too deeply engaging supplemental reading that we saw in The Nanny Ogg Cookbook.

Mrs Bradshaw's Handbook is a travel guide to following the route of the Ankh-Morpork and Sto Plains Hygienic Railway. If you have not yet read Raising Steam, you will be quite lost while reading this book. It covers towns and cities mentioned occasionally in Terry Pratchett's books but rarely delved into, providing a small slice of each. Here and there are amusing nuggets of humor and the odd joke, though the whole affair is written from the perspective of the well mannered Mrs Bradshaw.

If you've just discovered Terry Pratchett, give this one a pass until you've grown into a full fan. If you're an avid fan of Pratchett's, pick this one up and enjoy it for what it is.

For the Terry Pratchett fan who wants everything, this fun little book purports to be a facsimile of a railroad travel guide from the Discworld. It's whimsical and charming, but not completely necessary. The illustrations are particularly effective and Include humorous instructions from Moist Von Lipwick and the station environs. If you enjoyed the book Raising Steam (about the development of the Hygenic Railway), this little travelogue is a hoot. If you are a first time Pratchett reader, I don't think it is likely to appeal to you.
